Little Sprouts Literacy

Primary 📍 Prospect · Reviewed 18 April 2026
4.0 out of 5

The experience

Little Sprouts is run by Emma, a former junior-primary teacher, from a purpose-fitted room on Prospect Road that looks like the best corner of a very good classroom: decodable readers sorted by level, a phonics wall and not a screen in sight. Sessions are one-on-one and run for 45 minutes, structured around a systematic synthetic phonics sequence rather than the guess-from-the-picture approach some centres still use.

I watched a session with a Year 2 student who'd been flagged by their school for reading support. Emma moved through five short activities: sound review, blending drill, decodable reading, a dictation game and five minutes of pure fun reading at the end. The pace never let the student's attention drop, and every activity clearly targeted the same two sounds.

What stood out

The warmth is what parents mention first, and it's real. Kids visibly wanted to be there. But underneath the fun the program is rigorous: Emma benchmarks each child every ten weeks with a proper phonics screening and shares the results in a plain-English summary. Two families of children with dyslexia diagnoses told me Little Sprouts coordinated happily with their speech pathologists.

Room to improve

Demand is the problem. After-school slots are effectively full, with a waitlist Emma manages honestly but slowly, and several families were offered 3:45pm slots that don't work with school pickup. At $45 for 45 minutes it's fair for one-on-one specialist work, though noticeably pricier than group literacy programs. There's also no online option at all. Emma's view is that phonics work doesn't translate to a screen for this age group, which I respect, but it limits access.

Pros

  • Systematic phonics done properly, not guesswork
  • Ten-weekly benchmarking shared with parents
  • Genuinely warm: kids want to attend
  • Works well alongside schools and speech pathologists

Cons

  • Long waitlist for workable after-school times
  • No online option
  • Pricier than group literacy programs
4.0out of 5

The verdict

Exactly what early-reading intervention should look like: warm on the surface, rigorous underneath. Get on the waitlist early. It's worth it for any primary child who's fallen behind in reading.
