Lingua Adelaide (Mia Trentino)

Languages 📍 Unley · Reviewed 29 March 2026
4.5 out of 5

The experience

Mia teaches French and Italian from a study in Unley and over video, and her rule is famous among her students: from the moment you sit down, English costs you. Even absolute beginners are eased into target-language-only exchanges within a few lessons, heavily scaffolded with gesture, props and a shared vocabulary sheet, but real. The Year 11 French Continuers student whose lessons I followed went from rehearsed, robotic answers to something like actual conversation in about eight weeks.

Lessons are built backwards from speaking. Grammar appears when a conversation needs it, rather than as a worksheet sequence. The subjunctive turned up because the student wanted to say 'I wish my brother would…'.

What stood out

Oral exam preparation is the standout. Mia runs full mock orals under exam conditions, records them (with permission) and reviews the recording with the student line by line. Her feedback on pronunciation is precise without being discouraging. She's also unusually good over email: every lesson is followed by a summary with the new vocabulary and a two-line note on progress.

Room to improve

Students who thrive on structure may find the grammar coverage opportunistic. It follows the conversation, so gaps can persist until they happen to come up. A family whose son needed drilling for a school grammar test ended up supplementing with a workbook. Scheduling shifts occasionally (Mia travels to Italy each January, pausing lessons for three weeks), and her Saturday slots are perpetually full.

Pros

  • Students actually speak from lesson one
  • Outstanding SACE oral exam preparation
  • Excellent after-lesson summaries and vocab notes
  • Same warm method works in person and online

Cons

  • Grammar coverage is opportunistic, not systematic
  • Three-week January pause each year
  • Saturday slots effectively impossible to get
4.5out of 5

The verdict

The best choice in Adelaide I've found for building real spoken confidence in French or Italian, especially before SACE orals. Pair with a grammar workbook if your student needs systematic drilling.
