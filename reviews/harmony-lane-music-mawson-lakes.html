Harmony Lane Music Studio

Music 📍 Mawson Lakes · Reviewed 10 February 2026
3.0 out of 5

The experience

Harmony Lane is a three-room teaching studio near the Mawson Lakes interchange offering piano and guitar, mostly in 30-minute weekly slots. The teaching itself, when it happens as scheduled, is good: the piano teacher one family's student saw for term one was patient and technically precise, and got a nervous beginner through her first two AMEB pieces with real care.

The problem is everything around the teaching. In five months that same student had three different teachers. The first left for interstate, the second 'moved to Saturdays only' and each handover meant re-explaining goals and starting cold. Lesson reminders come from a booking system that twice showed different times than the front desk did.

What stood out

The teachers themselves are the studio's best asset: all working musicians, all personable. The studio recital in December was a lovely, low-pressure event that students clearly loved. Pricing is fair for the area at $40 per half hour, and casual learners who just want to enjoy an instrument will mostly have a good time here.

Room to improve

Reliability and communication need serious work. Beyond the teacher turnover, two lessons in five months were cancelled on under three hours' notice, and make-up lessons require chasing the front desk repeatedly. There's no practice tracking or parent communication between lessons, so progress depends entirely on which teacher you land and how long they stay. Families working towards AMEB exam dates should think carefully. Continuity matters for exam prep, and continuity is exactly what Harmony Lane struggles with.

Pros

  • Skilled, likeable teachers (while they stay)
  • Fair pricing for the northern suburbs
  • Lovely low-pressure student recitals

Cons

  • Three teachers in five months for one student
  • Short-notice cancellations, hard-won make-up lessons
  • Booking system and front desk contradict each other
  • No progress tracking between lessons
3.0out of 5

The verdict

Fine for casual learners who'll roll with a change of teacher; frustrating for anyone working towards AMEB exams. Until the scheduling and turnover settle down, it's hard to recommend for serious students.
