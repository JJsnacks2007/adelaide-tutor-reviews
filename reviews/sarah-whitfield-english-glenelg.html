Sarah Whitfield English & Essay Coaching

English 📍 Glenelg · Reviewed 2 July 2026
5.0 out of 5

The experience

Sarah works from a bright home studio a few streets back from Jetty Road, with online sessions for students further out. She's a former secondary English teacher and it shows: within twenty minutes of meeting a new student she had them talking, actually talking, about why their last essay felt flat, rather than sitting through a lecture on essay structure.

Her method is built around the redraft. Students submit writing before each session and it comes back covered in specific marginal comments: not 'expand this' but 'you've claimed the imagery is violent; quote the two words that prove it'. The session itself is then spent rewriting one section together, out loud. It's the closest thing to watching writing actually improve in real time that I've seen.

What stood out

The feedback quality is simply a level above every other English tutor I've reviewed. Her comments teach a way of thinking, and after a few weeks I noticed the student pre-empting them, self-editing in Sarah's voice. Two families I spoke to independently used the same phrase about their kids' results: 'jumped a grade'.

She's also scrupulously honest about scope. When a Year 9 parent asked about NAPLAN-style coaching, she referred them elsewhere rather than take the booking. That kind of integrity is rarer than it should be.

Room to improve

Almost nothing of substance. The honest caveats: her waitlist is real (I'd expect a term's wait for an after-school slot) and she doesn't tutor below Year 9. Sessions are firmly BYO-text, and students who arrive without having done the reading get a shortened session. Some families may find that strict. I think it's the right call.

Pros

  • Exceptional, specific written feedback on every draft
  • Redraft-focused sessions produce visible improvement
  • Former teacher who knows SACE marking from the inside
  • Honest about what she does and doesn't cover

Cons

  • Waitlist of a term or more for popular slots
  • Doesn't take students below Year 9
5.0out of 5

The verdict

The rare tutor who is worth waiting a term for. If your student's essays plateau no matter how hard they work, Sarah is the strongest English option I've reviewed anywhere in Adelaide.
