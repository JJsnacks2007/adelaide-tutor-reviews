Adelaide Science Hub

Science 📍 Adelaide CBD · Reviewed 21 May 2026
3.5 out of 5

The experience

Science Hub runs weekly group classes from a teaching space on Pirie Street. Chemistry, Physics and Biology, split by year level and capped (in theory) at eight students. The tutors are the real deal: the Stage 2 Chemistry class I observed was taught by a PhD student who explained equilibrium three different ways until every hand in the room went down. Content-wise, this is easily a four-and-a-half-star operation.

The 'capped at eight' claim is where things wobble. My observed class had eleven students, two sharing a bench in the corner, and the tutor, good as he was, couldn't get around to everyone during the worked-problems block. Two parents told me their term had classes of twelve.

What stood out

The teaching materials are excellent: each class comes with a printed problem set aligned to the SACE syllabus, with fully worked solutions released at week's end. Students who engage get something close to a second, better version of school. The group format also has a real energy to it, with questions from one student regularly unlocking something for three others.

Room to improve

Administration is the weak point. Class sizes drift above the advertised cap, emails about timetable changes arrive late (one family found out about a cancelled class from the locked door) and there's no per-student progress reporting at all. You'd have no idea how your child is tracking unless you ask them. At $49 a class it's good value for a confident, self-directed student, but a struggling student can hide in the group and no one will tell you.

Pros

  • Strong, engaging subject-expert tutors
  • Excellent SACE-aligned problem sets and solutions
  • Good value compared to one-on-one rates
  • Group energy suits confident students

Cons

  • Class sizes drift above the advertised cap
  • Patchy admin and late communication
  • No individual progress reporting
  • Struggling students can slip through unnoticed
3.5out of 5

The verdict

Great teaching wrapped in mediocre administration. Recommended for motivated students who'll ask questions and chase up material. Think twice if your child needs individual attention or you want visibility on their progress.
